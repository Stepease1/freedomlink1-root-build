# FreedomLink1 Governance Charter

**Adopted**: 2026-07-02  
**Status**: Genesis Constitution  
**Authority**: Founding Council & Community Consensus

## Preamble

We, the members of the FreedomLink1 community, do establish this Charter as the foundational governance framework for all operations, decisions, and amendments to our system.

## Article I: Principles

### 1.1 Transparency
All governance processes, records, and decisions shall be publicly documented and cryptographically verifiable.

### 1.2 Decentralization
No single entity holds exclusive authority. Power derives from community consensus documented in formal procedures.

### 1.3 Immutability
Once enacted, governance records become permanent and tamper-evident through cryptographic anchoring.

### 1.4 Accountability
All contributors are identified and credited in permanent registries. Actions are traced to their originators.

### 1.5 Consensus
Major decisions require documented agreement through established supermajority procedures.

## Article II: Governance Structure

### 2.1 Roles

**Founder Council**: Initiates and guides protocol governance  
**Contributors**: Participate in decision-making and implementation  
**Community Members**: Stakeholders with voice in material decisions  
**Arbiters**: Impartial parties handling disputes

### 2.2 Decision-Making Processes

- **Quorum**: 51% of active contributors present
- **Supermajority**: 66% approval required for amendments
- **Simple Majority**: 50%+ for operational decisions
- **Consensus**: Preferred; agreement without veto

### 2.3 Amendment Procedures

See `governance/amendment-protocol.md` for detailed amendment processes.

## Article III: Rights and Responsibilities

### 3.1 Contributor Rights
- Access to all governance documents and decisions
- Voice in consensus processes
- Permanent attribution and recognition
- Appeal procedures for disputes
- IP protection for contributions

### 3.2 Contributor Responsibilities
- Adherence to governance charter
- Good faith participation
- Compliance with cryptographic standards
- System integrity maintenance
- Documentation of actions
